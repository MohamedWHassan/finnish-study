Paikallissijat — Local Cases

Finnish uses local cases (paikallissijat) instead of prepositions like "from", "to", or "by". These are endings added directly to nouns. In the context of travel, three cases are especially important: Mistä? asks where from — it uses the elative case and marks the starting point (e.g. kotoa = from home, Helsingistä = from Helsinki). Millä? asks by what means — it uses the adessive case and marks the mode of transport (e.g. junalla = by train, autolla = by car). Mihin? asks where to — it uses the illative case and marks the destination (e.g. kotiin = home, kauppaan = to the shop). Together, a full sentence looks like: Hän lähtee kotoa junalla Turkuun — “She leaves from home by train to Turku.”
